<<<<<<< HEAD Top 10 Most Populated and Densely Inhabited Cities Around the World in 2024

In 2024, the most populated cities include Tokyo, Delhi, and Shanghai, with millions of people contributing to their rapid urban growth and development.

1. Tokyo, Japan

Population: 37.8 million
Along with being the most populous city, Tokyo is also a major international financial hub. It is home to the Tokyo Stock Exchange and many multinational corporations. The city is famous for its food scene, offering both traditional Japanese cuisine and international dining options. Tourists are drawn to attractions like the Tokyo Tower, Senso-ji Temple, and the vibrant district of Shibuya, home to the famous scramble crossing.

2. Delhi, India

Population: 32.9 million
Delhi is divided into two distinct parts: Old Delhi and New Delhi. Old Delhi is known for its historical monuments such as the Red Fort and Jama Masjid, while New Delhi houses government buildings and embassies. The city's metro system is one of the largest in the world, and its numerous festivals, including Diwali and Holi, make it a cultural hotspot. Delhi’s street food, like chaat and parathas, is also world-famous.

3. Shanghai, China

Population: 28.6 million
Shanghai is not only China’s economic center but also a growing global center for technology and innovation. The city’s Pudong district is home to the iconic Oriental Pearl Tower and the Shanghai Tower, the second tallest building in the world. Shanghai is also famous for its rich history, particularly its role as a center of international trade in the 19th and early 20th centuries. The city’s cuisine, especially xiaolongbao (soup dumplings), is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

4. São Paulo, Brazil

Population: 22.2 million
São Paulo is known for its influence in the arts, fashion, and entertainment. It hosts the São Paulo Fashion Week, the largest in Latin America, and has a thriving music scene, including samba and electronic music. The city is home to many museums, including the São Paulo Museum of Art, and it hosts the world’s largest gay pride parade. The city's ethnic diversity is evident in its neighborhoods like Liberdade (Japanese) and Bixiga (Italian).

5. Mexico City, Mexico

Population: 22.1 million
Mexico City stands out for its Aztec heritage, with the ancient Templo Mayor ruins located in the city center. It's also known for its museums, including the Frida Kahlo Museum and the National Museum of Anthropology. The city's vibrant culture is reflected in its colorful architecture, mariachi music, and Mexican cuisine like tacos and tamales. Despite its urban sprawl, Mexico City offers green spaces like Chapultepec Park, one of the largest city parks in the world.

6. Dhaka, Bangladesh

Population: 21.3 million
Dhaka is rapidly modernizing, with new skyscrapers and infrastructure projects underway, such as the metro rail system. The city is a center of South Asian culture, known for its music, traditional crafts, and literature. Despite rapid urbanization, Dhaka still retains its old-world charm in areas like Old Dhaka, where you can find the Ahsan Manzil and Lalbagh Fort. The Buriganga River plays a vital role in the city’s economy, with numerous ferries and cargo boats navigating its waters daily.

7. Cairo, Egypt

Population: 21 million
Cairo is not only the political capital but also the intellectual and cultural heart of Egypt. The city is home to the Al-Azhar University, one of the oldest universities in the world. Cairo’s markets, such as Khan El Khalili, are famous for traditional crafts, jewelry, and spices. Despite its chaotic traffic, the city continues to attract visitors with its rich history, vibrant arts scene, and bustling nightlife along the Nile River.

8. Mumbai, India

Population: 20.9million
Mumbai, often called the "City of Dreams," is the birthplace of Indian cinema, producing hundreds of films each year in Bollywood. The city is also home to important financial institutions like the Bombay Stock Exchange. In addition to its economic power, Mumbai has a deep cultural heritage, with colonial-era buildings like the Gateway of India and the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus. The city’s vibrant street markets and iconic street food, such as vada pav and pav bhaji, are a key part of daily life.

9. Beijing, China

Population: 20.4 million
Beijing’s rapid growth has made it a global city with a booming economy, yet it still preserves its historical and cultural treasures. The Great Wall of China, located just outside the city, remains one of the world’s most popular tourist destinations. Beijing is also known for its Peking Opera and rich culinary traditions, with dishes like Peking Duck being globally renowned. The city has also become a global center for technology and innovation, with rapid advances in AI and fintech.

10. Osaka, Japan

Population: 19.2 million
Osaka has long been a center of trade and commerce in Japan. It is often considered the culinary capital of Japan, famous for dishes such as okonomiyaki (savory pancakes) and takoyaki (octopus balls). The city’s historic Osaka Castle contrasts with its futuristic developments in districts like Umeda. Osaka is also a major port city and a gateway to the Kansai region, with easy access to cities like Kyoto and Nara.
