From Korean Dramas to Anime: How Global Streaming Trends Are Transforming Indian Entertainment Preferences in 2025

In 2025, India’s entertainment landscape is undergoing a dramatic transformation driven by global streaming trends. No longer restricted to Bollywood and regional cinema, Indian viewers have embraced international content with open arms. From the heart-touching romantic narratives of Korean dramas to the action-packed story arcs of Japanese anime, and even gripping Spanish and Turkish series, there’s a visible shift in what Indians watch and enjoy.

Major streaming platforms such as Netflix, Disney+ Hotstar, Amazon Prime Video, and Crunchyroll have adapted quickly. They now offer multilingual subtitles and dubs in popular Indian languages like Hindi, Tamil, and Telugu to cater to a diverse audience. Young people in metros and smaller towns alike are increasingly turning to K-Dramas and anime for both entertainment and cultural inspiration.

The popularity of these global genres has given rise to new social behaviors: anime merchandise stores, K-Drama fan meetups, Korean food pop-ups, and cosplay events are now common in cities like Mumbai, Delhi, and Bengaluru. Indian creators on platforms like YouTube and Instagram actively review and discuss these series, contributing to a growing digital ecosystem around global content.

This cultural shift is not merely about passive entertainment. It reflects India's growing global outlook and openness to international storytelling forms, indicating a broader social and technological evolution.

1️⃣ Korean Dramas: How K-Dramas Have Become a Daily Ritual for Indian Audiences

Once a niche interest, K-Dramas now occupy prime space in Indian living rooms. Their universal themes—love, revenge, familial bonds—resonate strongly with Indian viewers. What sets K-Dramas apart is their high production quality, compelling scripts, and shorter season lengths, making binge-watching more feasible. Indian streaming platforms have introduced curated K-Drama sections, with recommendations tailored for Hindi, Tamil, and Bengali-speaking audiences. Offline impact is also visible: K-pop music, Korean beauty products, and Korean language classes are gaining momentum.

2️⃣ Anime Craze in India: From Childhood Cartoons to Adult Fandom

Anime is no longer just a childhood memory for Indian millennials. Today, anime attracts viewers across age groups due to its complex storylines, intense character development, and visually stunning animation. Titles like Attack on Titan and One Piece have dedicated fanbases. Anime clubs in colleges, anime-themed cafes, and dedicated social media communities contribute to its growing cultural weight. Online retailers report high sales in anime-related merchandise such as figurines, clothing, and art prints.

3️⃣ Global Streaming Hits: Spanish Thrillers and Turkish Dramas Capturing Indian Hearts

Spanish crime thrillers like Money Heist and Turkish historical dramas like Diriliş: Ertuğrul have made impressive inroads into Indian households. Their gripping narratives and unfamiliar cultural settings offer Indian audiences a fresh perspective. Viewers no longer hesitate to watch subtitled content, breaking old habits. Platforms like MX Player and Netflix India frequently feature these shows on their homepage, underlining their widespread popularity.
